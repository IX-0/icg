# Story, Progression and Puzzles

Each stage is an island, although some are very similar. They all share the same shape.

## 1. The Rise

**Cenario:** Sandy, Daytime

The player gets a fade in animation and gets up.

There are 2 palm trees, some bushes and some light vegetation around the island. There are some litle rocks spread around as well. Somewhere around the middle there is a wooden throne. With 4 sockets for torches. There is a small wooden chest somewhere. There are 3 torches spread around the island. Beside one of the trees, a dead skeleton lies with a golden crown on its head.

**Puzzle:** The player needs to approach the dead skeleton beside the tree. Interacting with the skeleton allows the player to take the gold crown. Once taken, the skeleton turns into a scattered pile of bones. This pile of bones stays that way across all future islands. There will be a wooden throne where the player sits (whilst having the crown in its hand) and puts the crown on his head. Then a portal will appear in a boat comming from the sea. And a misty dense fog will start to form.

-> Skeleton (with Crown)
-> Crown
-> Skeleton turns to Bones
-> Throne (while holding Crown)
-> Boat

**Symbology:** I want to represent the power Napoleon had during it's reign. The throne and crown represent that power. The boat with the portal represents the seek for more power and the start of its story.

The portal disapears slowly after the player enters it.

## 2. The Isolation

**Cenario:** Gravel, Very misty day

The island is very similar to the *Rise*. This time, there is no bushes nor palm trees, some light vegetation and medium and big rocks in their place. The chest is in the same place as in *Rise*. The skeleton pile of bones is also in the same place beside the same tree.

**Puzzle:** The player needs to find a mirror, where it will realize that it lost its crown. Then the player will be prompted to find that lost crown. When returning to the mirror with the crown, the mirror will show the golden crown has a cardboard one. Throwing the crown in the mirror will shatter it, speeding up the game time and fading away the mist, the game stops at nighttime and a boat with a portal once again apeared from the sea.

-> Mirror
-> Crown
-> Mirror (while holding Crown)
-> Boat (while holding Torch)

**Symbology:** I want to represent the loss of authority and the realization that the newer one wasn't real. The cardboard crown represents the fake power that Napoleon had while in waterloo. Throwing the crown to the mirror represents the frustration of Napoleon and the boat apearing again represents the next step of his journey in the chase of power.

The portal stays there until the player passes holding a torch in its hand.

## 3. The Return

**Cenario:** Sandy, Nighttime

Exactly the same as in the *Rise*. So exact in fact, that if the player moved around the torches in *Rise*, they will be in the same position in *Return*. The chest will be in its persistent state (open if opened in *Rise*) and contains the flint and steel. The pile of bones remains where it was.

**Puzzle:** The player needs to find 4 torches and place them in 4 sockets on the ground. One of the torches is in the other side of the portal, so the player needs to go through the portal to get it, if it hadn't done so. With a flint and steel found on the chest, the player needs to light up the torches. When all torches are lit, all the torches and the throne will break apart and a "british" boat will appear from the sea with a portal.

These don't need to be made in order.

-> Torch from *Isolation*
-> 3 Torches spread around the *Return*
-> Sockets (while holding Torch)
-> Chest (Persistence)
-> Flint and Steel (found in chest)
-> Lighting up the torches
-> Boat

**Symbology:** I want to represent the return of Napoleon to France and the support he had from the people. The torches represent the regain in power. But it was short lived, as the boat represents the arrival of the british army and the start of his demise.

The portal disapears slowly after the player enters it.

## 4. The Tragedy

**Cenario:** Vulcanic sand, Nighttime

The vegetation is the same as in *Rise* and *Return*, there is a wooden house somewhere in the island. The torch sockets are there, but they are broken. There are no torches on the island.
There is a gardening hoe in the wooden shack. There is an X somewhere around the island.

**Puzzle:** The player needs to find the gardening hoe, use it to dig up the sand at the X, find a coffin below the ground. Opening the coffin will reveal a prompt to interact it (Saying: Go inside). There is an animation and the player is prompted to either leave the coffin or close it. Leaving does only does an animation leaving it. Closing it will fade out the players camera and fade in in the *Rise* and the game starts from the beggining.

-> Gardening Hoe
-> X 
-> Coffin
-> Close coffin
-> Restart Game
